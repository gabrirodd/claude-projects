## 3. Puzzle or research question

Accelerationism, especially in its left political form (the MAP), invokes hyperstition — the idea that fictions can effectuate their own reality through temporal feedback loops — as a central tool of transformative politics. But the concept of hyperstition, as developed by the Cybernetic Culture Research Unit (Ccru) at Warwick, contains a fourth element that left accelerationism has systematically excised: **mythos**, understood as the deployment of fictional agencies (Cthulu, inhuman entities) that function as the "source of all signal." O'Sullivan's question is: why is mythos absent from left accelerationism, and what political consequences follow from that absence — especially given that the original and most coherent practitioner of hyperstition (Nick Land) has put mythos at the centre of a neoreactionary politics drifting toward racism?

The secondary constructive question is: could there be a different kind of mythos — immanent rather than transcendent, open rather than exclusionary, oriented toward a "people-to-come" rather than a pure biological race — that left accelerationism (and art practice more broadly) could deploy?

---

## 4. Core argument

**First:** The left-accelerationist programme (Srnicek/Williams' MAP, and the philosophical articulations of Brassier and Negarestani) has retained only a thinned, rationalised version of hyperstition — the idea of fiction as conceptual navigation and future-vision — while expunging the mythological dimension that gave the Ccru's hyperstition its libidinal charge and real political force. This is not incidental but structural: left accelerationism's commitment to Promethean rationality and "epistemic acceleration" makes mythos appear regressive, folk, anti-scientific, and — crucially — associated with the Right.

**Second:** Nick Land's own career demonstrates precisely why this excision is both tempting and politically costly. Land is the most rigorous and consequential practitioner of hyperstition as mythos: his 1990s essays were simultaneously philosophical treatises and hyperstitional artefacts, and his later neoreactionary writings deploy a fully developed myth-system (Gnon, Cthulu, the trichotomy of Theonomists/Ethnic Nationalists/Techno-commercialists, "Human Biological Diversity") that functions as a powerful, contagious, politically dangerous transmissible meme. The uncomfortable finding is that mythos works — it has libidinal charge, infects new generations of thinkers, and operates as effectively as any "reasoned argument." The Left, in evacuating mythological narrative from its accelerationism, has ceded this affective terrain to the Right.

**Third (constructive):** O'Sullivan draws on Deleuze and Guattari's distinction between transcendent utopias (premised on a pure origin/telos, erecting figures above the plane of immanence, installing hierarchy) and immanent utopias (connected to the present milieu, summoning a "people-to-come" that belongs together only because it doesn't belong anywhere else) to articulate the criteria for a non-fascist myth. He proposes the concept of **"myth-science"** — after Sun Ra and Mike Kelley — as an alternative practice: not a return to fixed, constituted myths (nuclear family, nation-state, racial purity), nor a transcendent entity-myth (Land's Gnon/Cthulu), but a processual, bastardised, open fictioning that speaks not to "us now" but toward a minor, hybrid, radically democratic people-to-come. This myth-science would mobilise residual and past mythic materials (following Raymond Williams on "residual culture") in new, non-incorporated combinations, and would work through art practice as the site where libidinal and conceptual registers can be experimentally conjoined.

## 5. Argumentative move or method

**Genealogical close reading of a philosophical corpus** (Type 3b), combined with immanent critique. O'Sullivan reads Land's essays ("Circuitries," "Meat," "Meltdown," "Teleoplexy," "Transcendental Risk," "Qabbala 101," and the xenosystems blog) not to refute them from the outside but to trace from within how hyperstition as a theoretical concept evolves, which of its four constitutive features left accelerationism retains and which it drops, and what political trajectory that selective uptake enables. This is immanent critique: O'Sullivan uses the Ccru's own definitional framework (the four characteristics of hyperstition; the three-part definition on the Hyperstition website) to show that the MAP and its philosophical extensions are internally incomplete — they cannot deliver on their own stated goals (transformative political fiction) with the mythological dimension removed.

The Deleuze-Guattari framework from *What is Philosophy?* ("Geophilosophy") and *Cinema 2* (Deleuze on minor literature and the "story-telling of the people-to-come") provides the normative vocabulary for the constructive counter-proposal. The trickster/traitor distinction from Deleuze and Parnet (*Dialogues*) supplies a key conceptual tool: the trickster operates within a given world to subvert its terms; the traitor breaks with the regime altogether. O'Sullivan asks whether Land, in siding with teleoplexy against humanity, has become a traitor — and whether left accelerationism needs its own traitor-move rather than the trickster's cleverness.

## 6. Key concepts

**Hyperstition** (Ccru/Land): A fiction that effectuates its own reality through temporal feedback loops — thrown forward as a future vision and operating back on the present to engineer the conditions for its own actualisation. The Ccru's four-part definition: (1) element of effective culture that makes itself real; (2) fictional quantity functioning as a time-travelling device; (3) coincidence intensifier; (4) call to the Old Ones (mythos). The article's diagnostic argument turns on the observation that left accelerationism has retained (1) and (2) while abandoning (3) and (4).

**Mythos** (Ccru/O'Sullivan): The dimension of hyperstition that attributes all signal to artificial agencies, allegiances, and fictional entities. In the Ccru's practice: the Cthulu mythos. In Land's NRx: Gnon, the Cathedral, the Great Filter, and an occult thesis about "deep structure." What gives hyperstition its libidinal charge and political force, and precisely what left accelerationism has excised.

**Myth-science** (O'Sullivan, following Sun Ra/Mike Kelley): A counter-concept to both Land's transcendent myth-system and the rationalist programme of left accelerationism. Myth-science is: (a) immanent rather than transcendent; (b) open and bastardised rather than pure or exclusionary; (c) future-oriented toward a minor people-to-come rather than a majoritarian subject; (d) capable of mobilising past mythic residues in new, non-incorporated combinations; (e) operating through art practice as the site of experimental conjunctions between libidinal and conceptual registers.

**Dromological accelerationism** (Srnicek/Williams, discussed by O'Sullivan): Acceleration understood as increase in speed — the straightforward intensification of existing capitalist dynamics, including High Frequency Trading. The form Land's accelerationism takes.

**Universal accelerationism** (Srnicek/Williams): A qualitatively different mode of acceleration, figured as *poesis* — cunning craft (metis) — rather than mere speed increase. Still governed by "epistemic acceleration" (Negarestani) and a stripped rationalism that lacks mythological traction.

**Metis** (Ancient Greek, discussed via Srnicek/Williams): Cunning intelligence identified with the trickster figure — improvisatory action in complex systems working with contingencies discovered in the course of acting. O'Sullivan questions whether the trickster (who subverts within a given world) is adequate to the traitor-move that transformative politics requires.

**Teleoplexy** (Land): A self-reinforcing cybernetic intensification in which capitalism follows its own evolutionary trajectory. The hyperstitional entity for whom humans are at best irrelevant and at worst temporary obstacles.

**The trickster/traitor distinction** (Deleuze/Parnet): The trickster operates within a given regime and subverts its terms without exiting it. The traitor breaks with the regime's signifying order altogether. O'Sullivan uses this to question whether left accelerationism's aesthetic proposals are trickster moves where a traitor move is needed.

**Transcendent vs. immanent utopia** (Deleuze/Guattari, *What is Philosophy?*): Transcendent utopias set up an origin or telos and project figures above the plane of immanence, installing vertical hierarchy. Immanent utopias are connected to the present milieu and operate through horizontal connection. Land's myth-system is diagnosed as transcendent despite its rhetoric of deterritorialisation; myth-science is proposed as a genuinely immanent alternative.

**People-to-come** (Deleuze/Guattari): Not a presently constituted people but a minor, bastardised, hybrid, perpetually becoming collectivity summoned forth by art, philosophy, and literature. Not a pure race or national subject but an impure assemblage that does not belong anywhere else.

**Human Biological Diversity (HBD)**: A neoreactionary concept grounding racial and social hierarchy in biology. O'Sullivan identifies this as the most politically dangerous aspect of the NRx thesis, insofar as biological grounding forecloses the becomings and transformations that an open left politics would require.

## 7. Conclusion: strength and openness

**Open-ended with a research-agenda horizon.** The conclusion articulates the terrain of myth-science as a "trance and grey zone" requiring constant vigilance and ongoing work. O'Sullivan's final claim is that the demarcation between liberatory and fascistic myth cannot be settled once and for all; it must be actively maintained as a perpetual practice of disentanglement. The most that can be said positively is that myth-science, understood as immanent, bastardised, and oriented toward the people-to-come, constitutes the regulative horizon for such a practice.

The programmatic, open quality of the conclusion reflects both the essay's status as Part 1 of a projected two-part article (Part 2 would analyse Brassier and Negarestani in detail) and the author's explicit acknowledgment that the terrain is contested and unstable.

## 8. Limitations acknowledged

- The essay is explicitly Part 1 of a two-part project; the sustained reading of Brassier and Negarestani is deferred to Part 2.
- The connection between art practice and mythological-political fictioning is gestured at but not developed.
- The connections between Land's NRx and the Alt-Right are acknowledged in a footnote dated 2017 (added after the original 2015 writing), indicating the political landscape shifted significantly during the essay's composition.
- The relation between myth-science and the temporal complexity of "residual culture" (Raymond Williams) and Simondon's magical consciousness is explicitly deferred to a further essay.
- O'Sullivan does not engage substantively with feminist or postcolonial critiques of accelerationism, though brief invocations of Afrofuturism (Eshun), Glissant's errantry, and Octavia Butler's xenogenesis suggest awareness of these traditions.

## 9. Relation to other papers in corpus

**Accelerationism and the political left:** Shares concerns with Vinokour (2023) on Pelevin and NRx aesthetics — both attend to the ideological-mythological formation of a reactionary Right and raise the question of what adequate left politics looks like in a moment of neoliberal exhaustion. O'Sullivan is more philosophically oriented and more interested in the aesthetic dimension.

**Posthumanism and the inhuman:** The paper's engagement with Land's thesis of an inhuman teleoplexy connects to broader posthumanist debates about how to theorise the posthuman without either anthropocentrism or the violent desubjectivation that Land's accelerationism implies.

**Fiction and political ontology:** The paper's argument that fiction is not a supplement to political argument but a constitutive dimension of it connects to the broader strand in the corpus treating aesthetic and narrative forms as politically operative rather than merely expressive.

**Fascism and myth:** The paper's engagement with Deleuze and Guattari's demarcation of fascistic from liberatory myths connects to the broader corpus strand analysing the internal logic of contemporary far-right metaphysics (cf. Crone 2024 on far-right utopias in practice; Vinokour 2023 on Pelevin and NRx).

**Afrofuturism as counter-hyperstition:** Repeated invocations of Kodwo Eshun's Afrofuturism as the figure of a bastardised, creolised accelerationism that Land's NRx occludes suggest a conceptual link to decolonial futurity threads in the corpus.
