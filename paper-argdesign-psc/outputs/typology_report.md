# Argument typology — *Philosophy and Social Criticism* corpus

## Corpus overview

- **50 papers** analysed
- **Year range:** 2014–2026 (bulk 2022–2026)
- **Journal:** *Philosophy and Social Criticism* (PSC) exclusively
- **Intellectual tradition:** Critical social and political philosophy, broadly Frankfurt School-inflected but with strong analytic political philosophy and decolonial currents; recurring engagement with Habermas, Adorno, Fanon, Arendt, and Marx as central reference points

This corpus is philosophically denser and more normatively oriented than a comparable TCS or sociology corpus. PSC publishes at the intersection of Continental and analytic political philosophy, with a strong tradition of ideology critique, democratic theory, and increasingly decolonial and critical race theory. The absence of empirical-primary papers is striking: only one paper in 50 is classified `primarily_empirical_qualitative`, and even that (Çevik on Ottoman/Turkish religious pluralism) is in conference-proceedings format. The dominant mode is normative conceptual argument.

---

## Epistemic landscape

The PSC corpus is dominated by fully normative argument to a degree unusual even within philosophy journals. Over half the papers (28 of 50) operate entirely within conceptual and normative space, with no empirical evidence and no interpretive cultural case. A further six deploy philosophical texts, literary works, or contemporary events as interpretive cases rather than empirical evidence. Only nine papers bring qualitative empirical material to bear, and in several of these cases the empirical role is illustrative rather than constitutive. This epistemic profile reflects PSC's identity as a philosophy journal that takes contemporary social problems seriously while treating philosophical analysis as the primary method of inquiry.

| Epistemic mode | N | % |
|---|---|---|
| `fully_normative` | 28 | 56% |
| `normative_with_qualitative_empirical` | 8 | 16% |
| `purely_interpretive_hermeneutic` | 7 | 14% |
| `normative_with_interpretive_case` | 6 | 12% |
| `primarily_empirical_qualitative` | 1 | 2% |

What this distribution reveals about the field: PSC authors generally believe that normative conclusions require normative arguments. Empirical material, when present, typically serves as motivation or illustration rather than proof. The strong presence of purely interpretive papers (7/50) reflects the journal's commitment to intellectual history and philosophical genealogy as legitimate modes of philosophical inquiry. The relative paucity of interpretive-case papers (only 6, vs. 18 in the TCS corpus) reflects a disciplinary difference: PSC is less likely to use film, literature, or cultural objects as philosophical lenses than journals that bridge philosophy and cultural studies.

## Common epistemological ground

Across all 50 papers — regardless of typological classification — several presuppositions remain constant and largely unargued:

**Normative criticism requires theoretical justification.** Even the most empirically oriented papers in the corpus do not treat documented social harm as self-evidently warranting normative condemnation. Papers systematically justify their normative claims through argument, not merely by presenting evidence of suffering or injustice.

**Engagement with the existing literature is mandatory.** Every paper maps rival positions before advancing its own. This is not mere academic convention: it is the epistemological ground rule that normative claims acquire validity through contrast with alternatives. The rival_mapping move is structurally non-negotiable.

**Immanent critique is the preferred mode of disagreement.** When papers contest existing positions, they typically do so by showing internal inconsistency, failure to meet the rival's own standards, or suppressed implications — not by external normative challenge. This is the Frankfurt School inheritance: ideology critique works from within.

**The Frankfurt tradition provides the shared vocabulary.** Habermas, Adorno, Frankfurt School critical theory, and its interlocutors (Foucault, Arendt, Marx, Fanon) appear across virtually every typological cluster. This is not a citation convention: these thinkers supply the concepts (public sphere, reification, ideology, domination, recognition) that structure the arguments.

**Conceptual precision is a virtue.** Unlike cultural studies journals, PSC papers regularly use numbered definitions, careful conceptual distinctions (intrinsic vs. extrinsic institutional racism; idealist vs. materialist working-through), and formal argument structures. This analytic precision coexists with Continental theoretical depth.

## Type 1 — Special-issue introduction / research agenda frame

### Core logic (L1)
This type does not advance an individual argument so much as constitute a collective intellectual project: it maps a contested field, identifies productive tensions, and establishes the shared questions that subsequent papers will address. The normative stake is diagnostic and agenda-setting rather than thesis-driven.

### Standard move sequence (L2)
`puzzle_diagnosis → conceptual_distinction → framework_proposal → conclusion_open`

The opening establishes the field's live tensions; distinctions are drawn to clarify what is at stake; a programmatic frame is offered; the conclusion opens onto the work yet to be done.

### Detailed grammar (L3)
Type 1 papers are authored in the first-person plural or by multiple editors. They do not argue for a specific normative conclusion; instead, they enact a gesture of collective orientation. The puzzle_diagnosis is typically broad and structural (the public sphere is in crisis; deliberative democracy faces new challenges). The conceptual_distinction clarifies terms for the special issue's inquiry without resolving the questions. The framework_proposal introduces the conceptual resources the issue will deploy. The conclusion is necessarily open: closure would pre-empt the contributions the issue is designed to generate. No empirical case is required; the field situation itself is the object of diagnosis.

### Epistemic profile
- Primary mode: `fully_normative`
- Evidence role: absent
- Empirical methods: none
- Case type: none

### Canonical examples
**"The Public Sphere and Democracy in Transformation: Continuing the Debate — An Introduction" — Brunkhorst et al. 2023**
Why canonical: explicitly introduces a special issue; maps competing approaches to the public sphere; establishes the issue's questions without resolving them; research_agenda closure.

### Boundary with adjacent types
Type 1 is distinguished from Type 2 by its collective orientation and lack of independent thesis. Where Type 2 papers argue for a specific normative position, Type 1 papers establish the conditions for such arguments to be made. The absence of a conclusion_strong or even a conclusion_open-with-thesis is the key discriminant.

## Type 2 — Polemical normative reconstruction

This is by far the largest type in the PSC corpus (28 papers, 56%), and the analysis requires distinguishing three variants.

### Type 2a — Analytic reconstruction with strong thesis

#### Core logic (L1)
A clear normative thesis is advanced by systematically engaging and defeating rival positions through internal critique, then reconstructing the author's own position with precision and derivation.

#### Standard move sequence (L2)
`puzzle_diagnosis → rival_mapping → immanent_critique → framework_proposal → conceptual_distinction → normative_derivation → conclusion_strong`

#### Detailed grammar (L3)
The opening establishes a normative puzzle or contested question. The rival_mapping identifies and fairly characterises competing positions (usually 2-3). The immanent_critique attacks rivals on their own terms — not by asserting a counter-position but by showing internal inconsistency or failure to achieve stated goals. The framework_proposal articulates the author's own position with precision. The conceptual_distinction refines the framework by drawing boundaries (intrinsic vs. extrinsic; idealist vs. materialist). The normative_derivation draws out practical implications. The conclusion is strong: the argument has resolved the opening puzzle.

This variant draws most heavily on analytic political philosophy methodology: numbered cases, formal distinctions, internal objections.

#### Epistemic profile
- Primary mode: `fully_normative`
- Evidence role: absent
- Empirical methods: none
- Case type: none

#### Canonical examples
**"Against Institutional Racism" — Matthew 2022**
Why canonical: polemical thesis from the first paragraph; precise conceptual distinctions (S1–S4 scenarios, intrinsic vs. extrinsic institutional racism); systematic critique of Haslanger's structural approach; strong conclusion advocating return to individual-level analysis.

**"Freedom of Speech: A Relational Defence" — Bonotti and Seglow 2022** (variant)
How it deviates: less polemical framing; constructs a positive relational model rather than primarily attacking rivals.

**"False Consciousness, Hermeneutical Injustice, and Ideological Power" — Stahl 2024**
Why canonical: four-section dialectic structure; competing explananda identified with precision; each rival model shown to fail on its own terms; synthesis derived.

**"Understanding and Evaluating Populist Strategy" — Jenkins 2023**, **"Online Astroturfing: A Problem Beyond Disinformation" — Chan 2022**, **"The Appropriating Subject" — Cattien and Stopford 2022**, **"Racism, Epistemic Injustice, and Ideology Critique" — Bufkin 2024**, **"Contra Carl Schmitt" — Gómez Albarello 2025**, **"Why (and How) Statues Matter" — Stopford 2024**, **"Democratizing Civil Disobedience" — Celikates 2016**, **"Deep Disagreement as a Challenge for Democratic Education" — Albrecht 2025**

---

### Type 2b — Canonical thinker reconstruction for normative application

#### Core logic (L1)
A canonical thinker (usually from the Frankfurt School, Western Marxism, or post-colonial theory) is reconstructed and their thought is extended or updated for contemporary normative purposes. The reconstruction is not merely historical: it serves a present argumentative function.

#### Standard move sequence (L2)
`puzzle_diagnosis → rival_mapping → immanent_critique → genealogical_reconstruction → framework_proposal → normative_derivation → conclusion_strong`

The genealogical_reconstruction (or extended case_development) is the distinctive mark of this variant.

#### Detailed grammar (L3)
Opening establishes why the canonical thinker is relevant to a contemporary problem and why existing readings are inadequate. The genealogical_reconstruction offers a corrective reading: tracing the thinker's argument through their texts and identifying the dimension that standard interpretations have missed or suppressed. The framework_proposal extracts from this reconstruction a position that can be deployed in contemporary normative argument. The derivation extends this to practical implications. This variant is common in PSC because the journal has a strong culture of Ideologiekritik through intellectual history.

#### Epistemic profile
- Primary mode: `fully_normative`
- Evidence role: absent
- Empirical methods: none
- Case type: none

#### Canonical examples
**"Marcuse's Critique of Technology Today" — Feenberg 2023**
Why canonical: opens from Marcuse's contemporary neglect; reconstructs his technology critique against standard readings; derives implications for digital capitalism; conclusion_open (Marcuse offers tools but current situation exceeds his analysis).

**"Habermas and the Mutations of the Public Sphere" — Kellner 2024**
**"From Critical Theory to Critical Therapy" — Dyson 2024** (Fanon)
**"Socialist Democracy: Rosa Luxemburg's Challenge to Democratic Theory" — Muldoon and Booth 2022**
**"Expropriation of the Expropriators" — Blumenfeld 2022** (Marx)
**"Antonio Negri, Spinoza, Marx, and Digital Capitalism" — Fuchs 2025**
**"The Public Sphere in the Mode of Systematically Distorted Communication" — Kempf 2023**
**"Fugitive Freedom and Radical Care" — Loick 2023** (Black Radical Tradition + care ethics)
**"The Hegemonic World Picture" — Anastasiou 2025** (Laclau/Mouffe + AI)

### Type 2c — Conceptual critique and distinction without thesis application

#### Core logic (L1)
Papers that make their primary contribution through a conceptual distinction or corrective clarification, without deriving strong practical conclusions. The normative stake is the precision of the framework, not its application.

#### Standard move sequence (L2)
`puzzle_diagnosis → rival_mapping → genealogical_reconstruction → conceptual_distinction → framework_proposal → conclusion_open`

#### Detailed grammar (L3)
The opening diagnoses conceptual confusion or conflation in an existing debate. The genealogical move traces how the confusion arose. The conceptual_distinction is the central contribution: differentiating two concepts, paradigms, or uses that have been illegitimately collapsed. The framework_proposal restates the field with the distinction in place. The conclusion is open because the distinction itself is the contribution — it enables further inquiry rather than resolving a specific debate.

#### Canonical examples
**"Vergangenheitsbewältigung Revisited" — Schulz 2022**
Why canonical: identifies a conflation of two paradigms (idealist vs. materialist) in Habermas's use of Adorno's Aufarbeitung concept; the distinction is the paper's contribution; conclusion invites synthesis but does not execute it.

**"For a Negative Hermeneutics" — Giannakakis 2023**, **"The Hegel Test" — Bartonek 2026**, **"Foucault's Naturalism" — D'Cruz 2024**

## Type 3 — Interpretive case as normative lens

### Type 3a — Cultural object as critical parable

#### Core logic (L1)
A film, literary work, artwork, or cultural phenomenon is read philosophically as enacting or exemplifying a normative argument. The cultural object is not merely illustrative but constitutive: it generates insights not available to purely conceptual argument.

#### Standard move sequence (L2)
`puzzle_diagnosis → rival_mapping → case_introduction → case_development → conceptual_distinction → normative_derivation → conclusion_open`

#### Detailed grammar (L3)
The opening diagnoses a gap in existing theory. The case is introduced as a resource for filling this gap. The case_development is the philosophical reading: close analysis of the cultural object treats it as philosophical text. The conceptual_distinction articulates what the cultural object makes visible. The normative_derivation draws out implications. Closure is typically open because the case generates possibilities for inquiry rather than resolving a debate. The case is generative: concepts are derived from it.

#### Epistemic profile
- Primary mode: `normative_with_interpretive_case`
- Case function: `generative` or `constitutive_of_framework`
- Case medium: literary, film, art, philosophical text

#### Canonical examples
**"Patterns of Withdrawal: Adorno and the Beats" — Giannakakis 2026**
Why canonical: Adorno's theory and the Beat Generation are read together as mutually illuminating; each makes the other more precise; the case generates a normative argument about cultural withdrawal and critical consciousness.

**"Work of Art in the Age of Its AI Reproduction" — Kalpokas 2023** (Benjamin + AI art)
**"Jean Améry and the Time of Resentment" — Ferber 2023** (Améry's essay as philosophical text)

---

### Type 3b — Philosophical text as lens for contemporary diagnosis

#### Core logic (L1)
A philosophical text (rather than a cultural artifact) is read as a lens through which a contemporary problem becomes newly visible. The text is the medium through which a normative diagnosis is produced.

#### Standard move sequence (L2)
`puzzle_diagnosis → case_introduction → case_development → conceptual_distinction → normative_derivation → research_agenda`

#### Canonical examples
**"Towards a Decolonial Political Theory: Thinking from the Zone of Non-Being" — Des Portes 2024**
Why canonical: Césaire and Fanon's texts are read as philosophical resources for articulating a decolonial zone of non-being; the reading generates a normative framework for decolonial political theory.

**"Rethinking Utopias Through Hannah Arendt" — Firinci Orman 2025** (Arendt + youth climate activism)
**"Whistleblowing as Civil Disobedience: The Case of Edward Snowden" — Scheuerman 2014**

## Type 4 — Empirically anchored critical diagnosis

### Core logic (L1)
Qualitative empirical evidence (discourse analysis, historical case, institutional analysis) is deployed to diagnose a contemporary social pathology and derive normative conclusions. The empirical material is constitutive: the normative argument cannot be made without it.

### Standard move sequence (L2)
`empirical_presentation → puzzle_diagnosis → framework_proposal → case_introduction → case_development → normative_derivation → conclusion_open`

### Detailed grammar (L3)
The opening presents empirical material that demands normative explanation. The puzzle_diagnosis identifies what is theoretically puzzling about this material. The framework_proposal introduces the conceptual tools for the analysis. The case_development performs the empirical-theoretical analysis. The normative_derivation draws out what the analysis implies for theory or practice. Closure is typically open: the diagnosis is complete, but the political response remains to be worked out.

### Epistemic profile
- Primary mode: `normative_with_qualitative_empirical`
- Evidence role: `constitutive`
- Case function: `evidential_basis`

### Canonical examples
**"The Platformization of the Public Sphere and Its Challenge to Democracy" — Fischer and Jarren 2023**
Why canonical: opens with empirical evidence on platform concentration; derives a normative diagnosis of democratic threat; the evidence is constitutive of the argument.

**"The Platform Economy's Infrastructural Transformation of the Public Sphere" — Nosthoff and Maschewski 2023** (Cambridge Analytica / Facebook)
**"The Neoliberal Influence on South Africa's Early Democracy" — Fourie 2022**
**"Cin ciun cian (ching chong): Yellowness and Neo-Orientalism in Italy" — Miyake 2021**
**"Black Male Disposability and the Dark Side of Canadian Multiculturalism" — Ruwe 2026**
**"Loneliness and Radicalization" — Tirkkonen and Tietjen 2025**

### Boundary with adjacent types
Type 4 differs from Type 5 in the role of empirical evidence: in Type 4, the evidence constitutes the argument; in Type 5, it supports or illustrates a normative framework already established by other means. Type 4 also differs from Type 2 in that the puzzle is discovered through empirical observation, not generated from within an existing theoretical debate.

## Type 5 — Normative framework with empirical support

### Core logic (L1)
A normative framework is established through conceptual argument; empirical material (typically qualitative) plays a supporting role, either illustrating the framework's application or lending it credibility against objections.

### Standard move sequence (L2)
`puzzle_diagnosis → rival_mapping → framework_proposal → case_introduction → normative_derivation → conclusion_open`

### Canonical examples
**"Self-Esteem and Competition" — Gilabert 2023**
Why canonical: normative framework (self-esteem as social good undermined by competition) is established conceptually; empirical references to competitive social structures are supportive/illustrative.

**"Capitalism and Contested Publicity: A Conversation with Nancy Fraser" — Kempf and Sevignani 2023**

## Type 6 — Purely interpretive hermeneutic

### Core logic (L1)
A philosophical text or concept is reconstructed through close reading, genealogy, or intellectual history, without deriving an independent normative conclusion. The contribution is to correct understanding, not to prescribe action.

### Standard move sequence (L2)
`puzzle_diagnosis → rival_mapping → immanent_critique → case_development → counter_objection_response → conclusion_open`

### Detailed grammar (L3)
The opening establishes a misreading or interpretive gap that demands correction. The genealogy or close reading occupies the bulk of the paper. The conclusion is open: the correct reading is established, and further inquiry is made possible, but no prescriptive conclusion is drawn. This type is intellectually modest in the best sense: it resists the temptation to derive practical implications that the hermeneutic work does not support.

### Epistemic profile
- Primary mode: `purely_interpretive_hermeneutic`
- Evidence role: absent
- Case type: philosophical text or intellectual history

### Canonical examples
**"A Marxist Reading of Ludwig Wittgenstein" — Deegan 2023**
Why canonical: close reading of Wittgenstein §124 to argue it supports rather than forecloses social critique; no independent normative conclusion.

**"The Historicisation of the Human Senses: From Feuerbach to Marx" — Engelman 2023**
**"A Call for Psycho-Affective Change: Fanon, Feminism, and White Negrophobic Femininity" — Yokum 2022** (Fanon rehabilitation against Brownmiller)
**"A Semblance of Freedom: Horkheimer and Adorno's Conception of Myth" — Kent 2024**
**"If Nihilism is Murder, What Then? Camus' Distinctive Conception of Nihilism" — Sharpe 2025**
**"Nietzsche's Shadow: On the Origin and Development of the Term Nihilism" — Toribio Vazquez 2020**
**"Adorno's Negative Dialectics and Its Debt to Nietzsche" — Kastrinakis 2024**

### Boundary with adjacent types
The line between Type 6 and Type 3 is whether the paper deploys a cultural/philosophical object as a lens for normative argument (Type 3) or reconstructs it as an end in itself (Type 6). The boundary with Type 2b is whether the reconstruction is used to derive an original normative position for the present (Type 2b) or remains within the horizon of the reconstructed text (Type 6). Kent 2024 and Kastrinakis 2024 sit close to this boundary.

## Deviation map

### Papers that partially fit multiple types

**"Capitalism and Contested Publicity: A Conversation with Nancy Fraser" — Kempf and Sevignani 2023**
Bridges Type 1 (special-issue companion, dialogic/open orientation) and Type 5 (normative framework with illustrative empirical references). The interview format makes it a genre hybrid. Assigned Type 5 because the framing essay advances a normative argument about public sphere under capitalism; the interview format is the unusual vehicle.

**"Self-Esteem and Competition" — Gilabert 2023**
Bridges Type 2a (analytic normative reconstruction) and Type 5 (empirical support for normative framework). The qualitative empirical references are more than ornamental but less than constitutive. Assigned Type 5 given the empirical mode is explicitly invoked.

**"Rethinking Utopias Through Hannah Arendt: Youth Climate Activism and the Politics of Possibility" — Firinci Orman 2025**
Bridges Type 3b (Arendt as philosophical lens) and Type 4 (youth climate activism as empirical phenomenon). The Arendt reading is primary; the youth climate material serves as interpretive case rather than empirical evidence. Assigned Type 3b.

**"Whistleblowing as Civil Disobedience: The Case of Edward Snowden" — Scheuerman 2014**
Bridges Type 2a (analytic normative reconstruction of civil disobedience theory) and Type 3b (Snowden's statements read as philosophical texts). Assigned Type 3b because the Snowden case is generative — it advances civil disobedience theory rather than merely illustrating it.

**"Fugitive Freedom and Radical Care" — Loick 2023**
Bridges Type 2b (reconstruction of two philosophical traditions) and Type 2c (conceptual distinction building). The Black Radical Tradition and care ethics are introduced as philosophical sources; the contribution is primarily framework construction. Assigned Type 2b because the reconstruction of traditions is the dominant move.

### Papers that resist typology

**"From Empire to Nation: Management of Religious Pluralism in the Ottoman Empire and Turkey" — Çevik 2024**
The only `primarily_empirical_qualitative` paper in the corpus. The argument is historical-comparative rather than normative-with-empirical-support. The normative implications are present but minimal. Çevik is a genuine outlier: it belongs to a political science/historical sociology mode absent from the rest of the corpus. Its conference-proceedings format (Dublin Seminars 2023) explains the different register. It resists classification in any of the main typologies.

**"A Semblance of Freedom: Horkheimer and Adorno's Conception of Myth" — Kent 2024**
Sits at the Type 2b / Type 6 boundary. The paper reconstructs Horkheimer and Adorno's position and derives a positive claim about myth's emancipatory potential — but this claim stays within the horizon of the text rather than extending to normative prescription for the present. Assigned Type 6 but it is the most normatively ambitious paper in that cluster.

**"Why (and How) Statues Matter" — Stopford 2024**
Unusual in using analytic metaphysics (material constitution theory) to ground decolonial political conclusions. The methodology is analytic philosophy of metaphysics; the political orientation is decolonial theory. This cross-disciplinary move has no clear parallel in the corpus. Assigned Type 2a (analytic reconstruction) but its methodological profile is distinctive.

### What the deviations reveal

The deviations collectively reveal three live tensions in the PSC corpus:

**The empirical bridge problem.** PSC papers frequently invoke empirical examples to motivate or illustrate their arguments, but rarely commit to empirical analysis as a constitutive method. The papers that attempt the bridge — Tirkkonen-Tietjen, Ruwe, Miyake — stand out precisely because they take the risk of grounding normative claims in contingent evidence. The field has not settled on protocols for how empirical material should function in philosophical argument.

**The hermeneutic/normative boundary.** Several papers (Kent, Yokum, Kastrinakis, Sharpe) produce sophisticated readings of canonical thinkers but withhold the normative step of extending their findings to present prescription. This restraint may reflect philosophical scruple (the reconstruction is not sufficient to justify a normative claim) or methodological identity (the paper is intellectual history, not political philosophy). The tension between hermeneutic fidelity and normative ambition is not resolved.

**Interdisciplinary imports.** Three papers bring methods from outside philosophy — analytic metaphysics (Stopford), political science/comparative historical analysis (Çevik), and media theory/media studies (Nosthoff-Maschewski) — into a philosophical journal. These papers neither fully conform to PSC's philosophical norms nor to the norms of their home disciplines. They mark points where the journal's intellectual borders are under negotiation.

## Methodological notes

**Confidence levels by type.** Type 1 (1 paper) and Type 6 (7 papers) have the highest classification confidence — their epistemic profiles are clear and internally consistent. Type 2 (28 papers) has high confidence for the A and B variants; the C variant boundary with B is occasionally uncertain. Type 4 vs. Type 5 (constitutive vs. supportive empirical evidence) is the most difficult distinction in the corpus and several papers sit close to the boundary.

**Most uncertain profiles:** Kempf-Sevignani 2023 (interview genre), Loick 2023 (Type 2b vs. 2c), Firinci-Orman 2025 (Type 3b vs. 4), Scheuerman 2014 (Type 3b vs. 2a), Kent 2024 (Type 6 vs. 2b).

**What would strengthen or challenge this typology.** Including papers from adjacent journals (Constellations, Res Publica, European Journal of Political Theory) would test whether the PSC typology is field-specific or genre-wide. The very low rate of reflexive moves (2/50) may reflect PSC's argumentative culture — papers are expected to win their arguments, not qualify them — but this would need comparison with other venues to confirm. The absence of `normative_with_quantitative_empirical` papers is striking and probably reflects disciplinary rather than contingent factors.

**Year effects.** The post-2022 papers in the corpus show more methodological diversity (Tirkkonen-Tietjen, Ruwe, Stopford, Bartonek) than earlier papers, which cluster more heavily in the fully_normative mode. Whether this represents a trend or sampling artefact cannot be determined from this corpus alone.
