       Abstract                                              
       This essay presents the first systematic application of the Racial Subjugated Male Thesis
       (RSMT)toCanadianinstitutionsandtheCanadianMulticulturalismAct.WhileRSMThas
       beendevelopedintheU.S.context,Canadianpoliticalphilosophyhasnotyetengagedthis
       framework to understand how Canadian multiculturalism produces Black male dispos-
       ability.IarguethatdespiteCanada’sreputationformulticulturaltolerance,Blackmalesare
       ontologicallyconstructedasdisposablesubjectswhoseculturalnormsarepathologizedas
       incompatible with Canadian civic life. They are not simply misrecognized – they are
       rendered unrecognizable within the Canadian state. Through analysis of child welfare,
       education, employment, and criminal justice, I demonstrate that multiculturalism para-
       doxicallyproducesBlackmaledisposabilityasaconstitutivefeatureratherthanapolicy
       failure. This challenges liberal frameworks of minority rights and argues that addressing
       this disposability requires philosophical rupture.

      Introduction                                           
      In October 2016, the United Nations Working Group of Experts on People of African
      Descent visited Canada to assess the country’s progress in addressing racism and dis-
      crimination against African-descended people. Their findings were stark:
                                                             
        Despite Canada’s reputation for promoting multiculturalism and diversity and the positive
        measurestakentoaddressracialdiscrimination,theWorkingGroupisdeeplyconcernedabout
        thehumanrightssituationofAfricanCanadians…acrossthecountry,manypeopleofAfrican
        descent continue to live in poverty and poor health, have low educational attainment and are
        overrepresented inthe criminal justicesystem (UnitedNations2017,7).
                                                             
        As the Working Group found and this essay will detail, Black males in Canada face
      some of the worst outcomes in educational attainment; they are more likely to live in
      poverty;theyareover-representedinthecriminaljusticesystem,andtheyaresubjectedto
      disproportionate police violence. This raises an important question: what is the rela-
      tionshipbetweenBlackmaledisposabilityandCanadianmulticulturalism(Clarke1992;
      Clay-Gilmore 2022; Foster 1996; James 2021; Kitossa 2020; Walcott 2009)?

      Conclusion                                             
                                                             
      This essay has applied RSMT to the Canadian context for the first time, revealing that
      Black male disposability is not incidental to multiculturalism but is systematically
      produced through its institutions. What emerges from this analysis is that existing Ca-
      nadianpoliticalphilosophicalframeworksworkingwithinliberalrecognitionframeworks
      areinsufficienttotheproblem(Beiner&Norman2001;Jeffers,2013)Whatemergesfrom
      tracingtheunruly,unteachable,criminalincompetentphantomsacrossinstitutionsisnot
      four separate problems but a single ontological apparatus that ensures Black male dis-
      posabilityacrosstheentirelifecycleofBlackmales.Fromfostercaretoemployment,the
      samelogicoperatessuchthatindividualBlackmalesarerendereddisposablewithinthe
      civicinstitutionsbecausetheyarealreadyimaginedasembodimentsofphantomsthatdo
      not contribute to the civic multicultural polity of Canada.
        DespiteCanada’sglobalreputationasatolerantandinclusivemulticulturalsociety,the
      lived experiencesofBlack malesreveala profoundincongruencewiththe epistemesand
      axiological commitments of Canadian multiculturalism. Black males are not merely ex-
      cluded from civic institutions; they are constructed as beings devoid of the values and
      cultural norms deemed essential for the evolution of Canadian society. This exclusion
      manifests across education, employment, child welfare, and the criminal justice system,

       conceptofmulticulturalism,asenshrinedinCanadianlawandpolicy,promisesrecognition,
       dignity,andequalprotection,forBlackmales,thesepromisesremainunfulfilled.Instead,
       multiculturalismforBlackmalesoperatesthrough–medianarratives,policyframeworks,
       and institutional practices – that ensure that Black males are persistently imagined as
       disposable beings rather than seen as valuable contributors to national progress.
        Assuch,Blackmaledisposabilityoperatesinsuchamannerthatweareconditionedto
       accept it is normal that Black males are dead, unemployed, or incarcerated. This creates a
