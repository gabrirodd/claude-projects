      epistemiccompetenciestosupportmorerationaldeliberation(Warnick,2022).Students,it

      Conclusion                                             
      Thispaperbeganwiththeobservationthatdeepdisagreements–whicharedisagreements
      thatareparticularly difficulttoresolve andhighlylikely tointensifyconflicts–seemto
      pose a growing challenge to contemporary democracies. One potential response to this
      challenge could be democratic education, through which societies seek to equip future
      citizenswiththeabilitytoresolveconflictsdemocratically.However,Ihavearguedthat,in
      itscurrentform,democraticeducationscholarshipisscarcelyuptothistask.Althoughan

      educationthatthey initially setouttoreconsider.Thispaper hasattemptedtoshow that
      these standard liberal answers do not help us in cases of deep disagreement, as they
      presuppose the very ‘availability of common ground for reasonable debate and delib-
      eration’ (de Ridder, 2021, 226), which cannot be taken for granted in the case of deep
      disagreements.                                         
        For this reason, I have suggested that an education for democracy, if it is to prepare
      futurecitizenstodealwithdeepdisagreements,shouldassignagreaterroletocompromise
