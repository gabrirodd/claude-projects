# Jaros, Milan (2019) — The Episteme of Meta-Modernity: Order, Value, and Citizenship in the Space of 'Digital Finitudes'

**Zotero key:** PKV8J65F | **Date:** 2019-04-15 | **Collection:** First Review

## 1. Paper type: Theoretical — Type 2b (canonical thinker reconstruction) with Type 1 (programmatic/agenda-setting) character

**Move sequence:** `puzzle_diagnosis → genealogical_reconstruction [neoliberal complexification + Foucault] → rival_mapping [surveillance commoditism as the dominant pathology] → framework_proposal [meta-modern Foucauldian episteme] → normative_derivation [educational/management transformation] → conclusion_open/research_agenda`

---

## 2. Move sequence unpacked

The paper opens with a diagnosis of epistemic pathology: neoliberal complexification has produced a growing gap between emergent knowledge and human systems, such that most consequential decisions now occur in the "chaotic space of ephemeral price relations" — what the paper calls "surveillance commoditism." A genealogical reconstruction traces this pathology through the history of neoliberal division of labour and datafication. Foucault supplies the methodological resource (genealogy) rather than a specific conceptual apparatus — making this closer to Type 2b (Foucauldian method reconstructed for application) than to Type 2a. The framework_proposal — a "meta-modern Foucauldian episteme" with "order freed of power-hungry impositions" as its onto-epistemic variable — is the paper's main contribution, but it is stated programmatically rather than executed. The normative derivation calls for radical educational and management transformation. The paper has strong Type 1 character: incompleteness is constitutive, the conclusion is research-agenda, and it functions as a call to collective re-orientation rather than a resolved argument.

---

## 3. Puzzle or research question

The neoliberal division of labour, driven by digital complexification, has produced a growing gap between emergent knowledge and human systems — between knowing and being, between the human content of work and its measurable outcomes, between value as human capacity and value as price signal. The result: most consequential decisions are made within the "chaotic space of ephemeral price relations" manufactured by "surveillance commoditism." Can an alternative epistemic framework be constructed — an "onto-epistemic variable" — that grounds a rational basis for defeating "digitally enhanced serfdom" and restoring value, citizenship, and human independence?

---

## 4. Core argument

Advances in quantitative, empirical methodologies open an "action space" for a new research agenda: recasting past and present into *transparent, directional genealogical accounts of order generation and actualisation*. This is the proposed *meta-modern Foucauldian episteme*: a framework in which the concept of "order freed of power-hungry impositions" assumes the role of an onto-epistemic variable — a foundational unit of analysis through which knowledge production, value, and citizenship can be re-grounded outside the logic of price and surveillance.

Four interlocking claims:
1. Complexification, fuelled by neoliberal division of labour, has "acquired a life of its own" — generating the conditions for surveillance commoditism (colonisation of value by commodity-price relations mediated through data capture).
2. This epistemic colonisation is the key reason why the "lab & cloister" (sites of knowledge production) have been subordinated to the demands of "data-rich runaway" capital.
3. A Foucauldian genealogical approach — tracing the historical emergence of "order" as a principle of social organisation — reveals the contingency of current epistemic arrangements and opens space for alternatives.
4. The necessary condition for this alternative to assert itself is a radical methodological transformation of educational and management programmes — "aimed at bottom-up ownership of, and responsibility for the making, choosing, and symbolising" — restoring knowledge's "foundational mission of liberation by reason."

---

## 5. Argumentative move

Normative-genealogical: traces the epistemic crisis through the history of neoliberal quantification and datafication, then proposes an alternative episteme. The argumentative structure is philosophical-diagnostic: identify the pathology (surveillance commoditism, the gap between knowledge and value), trace its genealogy (neoliberal complexification), propose a corrective (meta-modern Foucauldian episteme with "order" as onto-epistemic variable), specify institutional requirements (educational/management transformation). No empirical data; evidence is historical-conceptual. The paper's language is dense and programmatic in ways that require inference to reconstruct specific claims.

---

## 6. Key concepts

- **Surveillance commoditism** (Jaros's proposed term): the hegemonic formation in which surveillance data is the medium through which all value is converted into price signal and fed into commodity relations — analogous to Zuboff's surveillance capitalism but emphasising the epistemic/value dimension rather than the behavioural futures market
- **Meta-modern Foucauldian episteme** (proposed): a new epistemic framework using genealogical method to recover "order" as an onto-epistemic variable outside the surveillance-commoditism complex
- **Order** (as onto-epistemic variable): "order freed of power-hungry impositions" — the paper's proposed foundational concept, a non-commodified measure of human actualisation; remains underspecified
- **Digital finitudes**: the constraints and determinations that digital infrastructure imposes on knowledge, value, and citizenship — historically contingent formations that genealogy can reveal and challenge
- **Lab & cloister**: sites of knowledge production (scientific laboratory + humanistic scholarly institution) both now subordinated to surveillance commoditism
- **Digitally enhanced serfdom**: the dystopian outcome the paper seeks to defeat — citizens reduced to data-producing serfs of surveillance capital
- **Bottom-up ownership**: the educational and organisational transformation required — subjects who actively participate in making and symbolising value, rather than receiving it as price signal from above

---

## 7. Conclusion: strength and openness

Normatively assertive in diagnosis; programmatically open in prescription. The diagnosis of surveillance commoditism and epistemic colonisation is advanced with confidence. The proposed meta-modern Foucauldian episteme is presented as a genuine alternative, but the "order freed of power-hungry impositions" concept is not sufficiently specified to function as the operative variable claimed. The conclusion is both a research agenda and a political-educational programme but lacks specified institutional pathways, mechanisms, or actors. The paper ends with exhortation rather than a plan.

---

## 8. Limitations acknowledged

Primarily implicit. The paper does not engage with existing critical data studies (Zuboff, boyd & Crawford), surveillance studies, or STS literature, limiting dialogue with the fields most directly concerned with the phenomena it diagnoses. The "meta-modern" concept is ambitious but underdeveloped and not clearly distinguished from existing uses of "metamodernism." The "order" concept as onto-epistemic variable is the paper's most original claim but also its least elaborated — at risk of functioning as an empty signifier. No empirical grounding is provided for the genealogical claims.

---

## 9. Relation to corpus

- Most directly aligned with **Baumgarten (2025)** in its programmatic-normative register and its call for a new critical framework adequate to the digital conjuncture. Both papers are prolegomenary rather than executory.
- "Surveillance commoditism" extends and radicalises **Rieder & Simon (2015)**'s diagnosis: where Rieder & Simon focus on the institutional failure of accountability mechanisms, Jaros argues the epistemic framework itself is colonised and requires replacement.
- Connects to **Amaral et al. (2025)** on neoliberal rationality as the structural condition of digital power, and to **Hu (n.d.)** on digital infrastructure co-producing governance forms.
- The genealogical method and Foucauldian toolkit are shared with **Hu** and **Amaral** — all three use genealogy, but Jaros applies it at the broadest epistemic level (the concept of "order" itself) rather than to specific political formations.
- The "lab & cloister" concept and the emphasis on educational transformation are unique in the corpus, pointing toward an institutional dimension the other papers do not address.
